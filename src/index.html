Warrior II

Also, known as Virabhadrasana II in Sanskrit.

This pose is a powerful standing pose that strengthens the legs, hips, and core while opening up the chest and shoulders. It is typically performed with the front foot facing forward and the back foot turned out at a 90-degree angle, with the arms extended out to the sides and the gaze focused over the front hand.

The pose is named after the mythological warrior Virabhadra, who was created by the god Shiva from a lock of his hair.As with any yoga pose, it's important to listen to your body and modify the pose as needed to suit your individual needs and abilities.

Tree Pose

(Vrksasana in Sanskrit)

This pose is a balancing pose that strengthens the legs, ankles, and core while improving focus and concentration. It is typically performed with one foot placed on the inner thigh of the opposite leg, while the arms are extended overhead with the palms pressed together.

Chakrasana

Chakrasana, the Bridge Pose, stands as a testament to the fluidity of existence.

As you lay upon your back, surrendering to the earth's embrace, your hands and feet become the pillars of connection between heaven and earth. With each breath, you ascend, arching your spine towards the heavens, embracing the energy that flows freely through your being..

You can achieve this posture by following the steps provided by yoga.omgyan.com/.

Plow Pose

The Plow Pose, also known as Halasana in Sanskrit, is a rejuvenating yoga posture that embodies both grace and strength.

In this pose, practitioners lie on their backs and lift their legs overhead, allowing their feet to touch the ground behind their head. The body forms a shape resembling a plow in a field, hence the name. Halasana stimulates the spine, stretches the back muscles, and promotes relaxation.

It is often practiced towards the end of a yoga session to calm the mind and prepare for restorative poses or meditation. finessyoga.com.

High Plank

High Plank, also known as Phalakasana or Kumbhakasana, is a foundational yoga pose that strengthens the core, arms, shoulders, and wrists.

To practice High Plank, begin in a push-up position with the wrists aligned under the shoulders, fingers spread wide, and the body forming a straight line from head to heels. Engage the core muscles and press the floor away with the hands, maintaining a strong and stable posture.

Hold the position for several breaths, building strength and endurance while cultivating focus and stability in both body and mind. yogabasics.com.

Double Dog Pose B

The Double Dog Pose, also known as Dwi Pada Viparita Dandasana in Sanskrit, is a dynamic yoga posture that embodies strength, balance, and expansion.

In this pose, practitioners elevate themselves into a reverse plank position with both feet and hands grounded firmly on the mat, creating an inverted V shape with the body. The Double Dog Pose offers a deep stretch to the shoulders, chest, and spine while simultaneously engaging the core muscles and promoting stability and focus.

It symbolizes the union of dualities within oneself, inviting practitioners to explore their inner strength and balance with grace and intention. tummee.com.
